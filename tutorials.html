Classic Paper Crane

Beginner

Learn to fold the most iconic origami model. The paper crane symbolizes peace and good fortune in Japanese culture.

Materials Needed:

One square piece of paper (6" x 6" recommended for beginners)

Basic Steps:

  1. Start with a square base fold
  2. Create the preliminary base
  3. Form the bird base
  4. Pull out the head and tail
  5. Shape the wings and inflate the body

Jumping Frog

Intermediate

Fold a frog that actually jumps! This interactive model is perfect for entertaining children.

Materials Needed:

Rectangular paper (8.5" x 11" cut in half works well)

Basic Steps:

  1. Create diagonal creases on top half
  2. Form the frog's head section
  3. Shape the body with valley folds
  4. Create the jumping mechanism
  5. Test and adjust the spring action

Kawasaki Rose

Advanced

Master this complex and beautiful rose design created by Toshikazu Kawasaki. A true test of origami skills.

Materials Needed:

Large square paper (10" x 10" minimum), patience, and practice

Basic Steps:

  1. Create complex crease pattern
  2. Pre-fold all valley and mountain creases
  3. Collapse along the twist pattern
  4. Shape the spiral center
  5. Form and curl the outer petals

Modular Star

Intermediate

Learn modular origami by creating this stunning geometric star made from multiple identical units.

Materials Needed:

6 squares of paper in different colors (same size)

Basic Steps:

  1. Fold 6 identical modules
  2. Learn the interlocking technique
  3. Assemble modules in pairs
  4. Connect all units together
  5. Lock the final connection
